About the Harmonium

Harmonium is the most vital instrument for a Classical Indian Singer. This helps the vocalist match his pitch to the proper tone. It is known more commonly as a Reed Organ or a Pump Organ. In the harmonium, is a vacuum of empty space. Air is pumped into this, and is released through different sized metal chutes. This creates the sound at a certain pitch.

There is a controversy on the origin of the pump organ, but the most accepted idea is that it was created in England, during the mid-19th Century. It was designed to be used for various types of music. It has knobs on the front which allow you to change the octave, and plays like a piano with the key layout. Due to its versatility and harmonious sound, it is the main musical instrument for accompanying any performance.
