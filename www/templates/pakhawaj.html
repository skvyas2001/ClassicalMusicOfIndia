About the Pakhawaj

The Pakhawaj was the original percussion instrument used for Indian Classical Music. It is said to be the mother of all Indian Drums. Along with it's musical history, it has many religious implications as well. It is said to be the musical instrument of Lord Ganesha.

It looks very similar to the Mridangam, which is the most prominent percussion instrument of South India. The Pakhawaj is in a barrel shape and has 2 heads on the opposite sides. The bass side has a mixture of flour and water applied to it before use and the other side has a large black spot. The lacing is for tension and stability and the wooden cylinders are used to tune the Pakhawaj. It makes sounds such as "Tita Kata" and "Gadi Gana".
